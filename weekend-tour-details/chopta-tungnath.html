🏔️ Overview

A complete Chopta & Tungnath experience

The Chopta, Tungnath, and Deoriatal Trek is one of the most stunning trekking routes in Uttarakhand, India, offering trekkers a blend of spirituality, adventure, and nature. The trek is not only visually captivating but also holds immense religious significance.

Main Stops

Devprayag, Chopta, Tungnath, and Chandrashila.

Main Draw

Trekking, Camping, and High Altitude Temple.

Best For

Trekkers, Backpackers, and Nature Lovers.

Pace

Adventurous & Moderate Trek.

Alternate Route and Trekking Journey

  • Start Point - Delhi to Chopta: The trek kicks off from Delhi, with a drive towards Chopta, where the trek to Tungnath begins. The route passes through lush green meadows, forests, and small villages.
  • Chopta to Tungnath: The trek from Chopta to Tungnath is easy to moderate. The path is well-defined, passing through alpine meadows and forests, giving trekkers an unforgettable Himalayan experience.
  • Tungnath to Chandrashila: For those looking for a challenge, extending the trek to Chandrashila Peak is an excellent option. This part of the trek involves a steep climb but rewards trekkers with a view of the Himalayan peaks and valleys.
  • Tungnath to Deoriatal: After completing the trek to Tungnath and Chandrashila, trekkers return to Chopta and head toward Deoriatal. This route offers an easy descent and is about 2-3 hours long.
  • Deoriatal to Sari Village: After spending time at Deoriatal, trekkers descend towards Sari Village, where the trek concludes. It’s a relatively short downhill trek.

Must-Visit Places Around the Trek

  • Chopta: Known as “Mini Switzerland,” Chopta offers panoramic views of the Himalayas.
  • Tungnath Temple: As one of the highest temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple’s significance is both spiritual and historical. It’s also part of the revered Panch Kedar pilgrimage circuit.
  • Deoriatal Lake: A serene spot ideal for nature lovers and photographers. The reflections of snow-clad peaks in the lake are breathtaking.
  • Chandrashila Peak: A trek to this summit offers an unmatched 360-degree view of the Himalayas, including famous peaks like Nanda Devi and Trishul.
  • Ukhimath: This town is a center of spirituality and is the winter home for the idols of Kedarnath Temple.
  • Sari Village: A picturesque village that serves as the base for the Deoriatal trek. It’s a traditional Himalayan village.
  • Rudraprayag: Located at the confluence of the Alaknanda and Mandakini rivers, this town is known for its temples and spiritual significance.

Activities to Enjoy During the Trek

  • Trekking: Through lush forests, across meadows, and towards majestic Himalayan peaks. It’s an adventure filled with spectacular views at every step.
  • Camping: Camping under the stars in Chopta offers a unique experience with the cool mountain breeze.
  • Photography: With its breathtaking vistas of towering peaks, verdant meadows, and crystal-clear lakes.
  • Nature Walks: The serene environment is perfect for nature walks around Deoriatal Lake or through the forests surrounding Chopta.
  • Bird Watching: The area is home to various species of birds, making it an ideal spot for bird watchers.
  • Spiritual Experience: Visiting Tungnath Temple and Chandrashila Peak offers a spiritual journey for those seeking a connection with divinity.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal months for trekking Chopta, Tungnath, and Deoriatal are March to June (summer months) and September to November (post-monsoon). Winter months can be a challenge due to heavy snowfall.
